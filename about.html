About the Project

Project Overview

The Telangana Water Quality Monitoring Framework is a comprehensive system for visualizing and analyzing water quality across 213 monitoring stations in Telangana state. This dashboard combines geospatial visualization with real-world environmental data to provide actionable insights.

Data Source

Provider: Telangana Pollution Control Board (TPCB)
Period: June 2025
Coverage: 213 monitoring stations across major rivers (Godavari, Krishna, Musi, Manjeera) and urban/rural lakes and tanks
Parameters: 18 physical, chemical, and biological indicators including pH, DO, BOD, COD, coliform bacteria, TDS, and more

Methodology

Water Quality Index (WQI) Calculation: Based on WHO and BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) drinking water quality guidelines. Each parameter is weighted according to its health significance and combined into a composite score (0-100).

Classification:

  • Safe/Potable (WQI â‰¥ 70): Suitable for drinking with basic treatment
  • Polluted (WQI 45-69): Requires advanced treatment before use
  • Highly Polluted (WQI < 45): Not suitable for most uses without extensive treatment

Key Findings

Analysis of June 2025 data reveals:

  • Overall Status: Average WQI of 46.20 (Moderate Pollution)
  • Safe Water: 33.6% of stations meet high quality standards
  • Polluted: 10.7% require treatment before use
  • Highly Polluted: 55.6% are severely contaminated
  • Most Affected: River Musi in urban Hyderabad, urban lakes with sewage inflow

Technology Stack

  • Leaflet.js - Interactive mapping library
  • GeoJSON - Spatial data format
  • HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript - Frontend technologies
  • WHO & BIS Standards - Water quality classification
